About MediHacks

MediHacks is an organization dedicated to hosting events to foster and recognize talent and innovation in the field of medical technology. We have hosted 3 virtual hackathons with over 1,200 total participants, giving out thousands of dollars, pounds, and rupees in cash to hackers across the globe.

MediHacks events are a typically 3-7 day events where participants from all over the world build websites, mobile apps, and other software and hardware projects that solve a problem relating to the event's theme. After the event, a team of judges score the projects and provide feedback addressing both the technical and business ends of the product. Cash and other prizes are sent promptly in the following days.

  • Compete with other hackers
  • Develop your skills
  • Boost your resume
  • Win awesome prizes

Featured Projects

Across three events with over 1,200 total participants, more than 100 projects have been submitted to MediHacks events from participants of all ages, ethnicites, and backgrounds. These are some of our favorites!

LifeLink | 1st Place, MediHacks 2024
Democratizing a first-responder-network for third-world countries through an AI-powered software-hardware combination. Eric Li & Gautham Venkateshwaran | University of Waterloo, University of British Columbia
TrackCat | Honorable Mention, MediHacks 2023
Track Cat is an app that allows students to anonymously track their daily emotions, enabling schools to identify and address issues like self-harm, bullying, and burnout to support mental health. Brissa Valdez, Juan Salazar, Blanca Perez, Jorge Quintero | CETYS University, Mexico
MedGuard | 1st Place Under 18 Division, MediHacks 2024
MedGuard is an elderly assistance app that enhances healthcare by providing features like medication reminders, a disease detector, personalized health tips, accessible design, and emergency SOS alerts to support users' health and safety. Malay Patel | Fountainhead School, India
LetMeCook | Best High School Hack, MediHacks 2023
Snap your fridge, discover a recipe. A computer vision mobile app that helps you find personalized recipes based on your ingredients and dietary needs. Kevin Liu, Arjun Tschand, Animish Jain, Sharabh Ojha | High Technology High School, New Jersey
